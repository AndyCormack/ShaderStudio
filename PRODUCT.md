# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are shader developers, technical artists, and graphics programmers working on effects for Unreal Engine and the web. They use shader-studio locally alongside their normal IDE and need a fast, low-friction way to develop, preview, tune, organize, and rediscover portable shaders.

## Product Purpose

shader-studio is a local-first look-development workspace for rapidly iterating on a shader's appearance and algorithm in portable GLSL. It provides immediate visual feedback for fullscreen website effects and mesh-based game effects, while preserving a focused IDE-first workflow. As the shader collection grows, the gallery, search, tags, and filtering should make existing work quick to find and reuse.

Success means moving from an idea to a convincing, reusable effect quickly, without the studio becoming the slow or distracting part of the workflow.

## Positioning

One local workspace to create, preview, tune, organize, and rediscover portable shaders for both web and Unreal-oriented work.

## Brand Personality

Professional, focused, and user-friendly: a calm professional instrument with an electric edge. The interface should have the clarity and approachability of Rive, the durable workspace structure of PlayCanvas, the discovery strengths of Shadertoy, and the distinctive personality of ComfyUI Desktop. Quiet application chrome should let shader output carry most of the spectacle; identity comes from a recognizable accent, precise motion, polished feedback, and a distinctive mark.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic SaaS dashboard, a dense Blender or Unity cockpit, an RGB-gamer cyberpunk interface, or a bubbly creative toy. Avoid hazy low-contrast treatments, decorative marketing-page scaffolding, and visual effects that compete with the shader being evaluated.

## Design Principles

1. **Keep the shader primary.** The rendered effect is the workspace's focal point; application chrome stays calm, compact, and subordinate.
2. **Protect the iteration loop.** Common actions and feedback should be immediate, predictable, and require minimal navigation.
3. **Design for the growing collection.** Live previews, search, tags, and useful filters make rediscovery a core workflow rather than an afterthought.
4. **Earn personality through precision.** Use a distinctive accent, mark, motion language, and feedback states without decorating every surface.
5. **Prefer familiar, accessible controls.** Professional capability should not require users to decode unusual interaction patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard operation, clearly visible focus states, status cues that do not rely on color alone, sufficient text and control contrast, and reduced-motion preferences.
